Start your story.

Your first night should feel exciting, not confusing. Follow the guide from arrival to your first job, first collection and first place to call home, then let the city open naturally around you.

Your first session

Five clear steps after landing.

GLRP is built so new players can learn by doing. You do not need to understand every system immediately—focus on these first actions and the rest of the city opens naturally.

Start here
Minute 01Finish your characterCreate a believable identity, outfit and direction that fits serious roleplay.
Minute 03Check your starter homeLearn the apartment controls, storage areas and wardrobe before heading out.
Minute 05Open your starter gearFind your backpack, phone, card box and scanner so you know what tools you already own.
Minute 08Read your Life PathYour opening objectives give you a reason to explore and a clear route toward better opportunities.
Minute 12Enter the cityUse your phone, find legal starter work, meet players or begin your first street-level task.
Best first move: keep your starter tools safe, read the phone and Life Path screens, and ask questions in character where possible.

Character progression

Life Paths turn actions into reputation.

Life Paths give characters themed objectives, XP and unlocks. Early progression starts at street level before deeper specialist paths become available.

Daily & long-term

How progression works

  • Choose or begin the available starter route.
  • Complete daily and milestone objectives.
  • Earn path XP from real gameplay actions.
  • Unlock perks, specialist tasks and stronger opportunities.
  • Build a character identity through the work you actually do.

Examples across the city

  • Bins, recycling and street survival.
  • Mechanic diagnostics, repairs and road calls.
  • Deliveries, businesses and supplier progression.
  • Creature surveys, collecting and care.
  • Future legal, criminal and specialist paths.
Fresh goals every day are paced. Once a daily task is completed, move on to another activity instead of repeating one action endlessly.
Collectible gameplay

Open packs. Scan districts. Build a team.

GLRP combines interactive card packs with a creature-catching system. Both are designed as long-term collections rather than one-time menus.

Collect & compete

Card packs

  • Use the interactive pull-tab opening experience.
  • Reveal different rarities and collection pieces.
  • Keep early pulls inside your collection flow.
  • Build toward trading, showcases and future competition.

Creature catching

  • Use the scanner to find district signals.
  • Capture creatures with different stats and rarity.
  • Build a team, train favourites and explore evolution.
  • Use duplicates and eggs as part of long-term progression.
Explore instead of standing still. Creature signals, surveys and collection opportunities are designed to send players through different districts.
Apartments & storage

Your backpack travels. Your home keeps the rest safe.

Portable storage and apartment storage have different jobs. Learning that difference early keeps your inventory clean and stops important items being carried everywhere.

Keep organised

Backpack

  • Use it for items you need while travelling.
  • Keep job tools and everyday supplies organised.
  • Different backpack types may offer different storage value.
  • Avoid filling portable space with items that belong at home.

Apartment tabs

  • Storage keeps spare items and long-term supplies at home.
  • Wardrobe access handles saved outfits and character clothing.
  • Home controls give you a safe base before entering city life.
  • Future properties can expand how your character lives and stores belongings.
Simple rule: carry what your current scene needs and store the rest. This makes jobs, searches and inventory management much easier.
Community support

Get help without breaking the scene.

GLRP supports new and experienced players. Use in-character help for normal city questions and official support channels for technical issues, applications or rule concerns.

Discord open

  • Learning where legal work and public locations are.
  • Meeting people and asking believable city questions.
  • Building scenes instead of treating everything like a game menu.

  • Technical problems or missing items.
  • Rule questions and staff reports.
